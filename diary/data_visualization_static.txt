Milestone 2 - Data Visualization

Date: 19 Mar 2026
How did you design your dashboard and what did you learn from visualizing the results?
    This week we built the static dashboard notebook using matplotlib and ipywidgets. The dashboard has 7 static views and 3 interactive widgets.
    Static Views:
        1. Global Electricity Access Trend (1990–2023) — line chart showing global average rising from ~72% to ~90%, plus a histogram of the most recent year's distribution
        revealing the bimodal pattern (many near 100%, many stuck below 50%).

        2. Risk Category Distribution — bar chart of overall counts plus a line chart showing how Severe/Moderate/Minimal countries changed over time. The sharp decline in
        Severe countries after 2010 reflects major electrification pushes in South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa.

        3. Electricity Access Progress (1990–2023) — top 10 most improved countries by percentage point gain, and the 15 countries with the lowest access in the most
        recent year. Dark red bars highlight countries below 30% — the most critical cases.

        4. Feature Importance — side by side bar charts for the best regressor (RF Improved) and best classifier (RF Baseline). Both models agree that renewable_energy_percent
        and gdp_per_capita dominate predictions.

        5. Correlation with Electricity Access — Pearson correlation bar chart showing rural_population_percent as the strongest negative driver and
        renewable_energy_percent and gdp_per_capita as the strongest positive drivers.

        6. Model Performance Comparison — bar charts comparing all 4 models on validation accuracy (classification) and R² (regression). RF Baseline won classification at
        0.9297 and RF Improved won regression at R²=0.9178.

        7. Test Set Evaluation — actual vs predicted scatter for regression and confusion matrix for classification on the 2021–2023 test set. Classifier achieved 87.77%
        test accuracy. Moderate class was hardest to predict — it is the transition zone.

        8. GDP vs Electricity Access — scatter plot across four reference years (1995, 2005, 2015, 2023) showing the positive relationship between economic development and
        electricity access. Countries have moved upward and rightward over time.

        9. 2024–2027 Forecast — grouped bar chart of predicted risk categories per year and a combined historical + forecast line chart. Global average projected to rise from
        85.8% (2024) to 86.3% (2027). 27 countries remain Severe by 2027, almost entirely in Sub-Saharan Africa.

    Interactive Widgets:
        - Widget A (country dropdown): shows any country's full trajectory — historical actual, test predictions (2021–2023), and forecast (2024–2027) side by side with GDP.
        - Widget B (year slider): year-by-year global snapshot of GDP vs electricity access coloured by renewable energy % — the two strongest predictors from feature
        importance together in one chart.
        - Widget C (risk transition range slider): transition matrix showing how countries moved between Severe/Moderate/Minimal between any two selected years, plus a
        list of which Severe countries improved and which remain stuck.

Key design decisions:
1. Added ipywidgets fallback static plots for environments where widgets don't render (e.g. VS Code).
2. Widget B uses renewable_energy_percent as the colour dimension to stay consistent with feature importance findings — GDP on X axis, renewable
   energy as colour, both top predictors visible in one chart.
3. Widget C uses a risk transition explorer — directly tied to the project goal of identifying which countries face persistent energy poverty risk.

Key finding communicated by the dashboard:
Global electricity access has improved substantially since 1990 but progress is slowing. The 27 countries projected to remain in Severe risk by 2027 share the same
structural barriers — low GDP, high rural population, weak institutions — and will require targeted policy intervention beyond what trend extrapolation predicts.
