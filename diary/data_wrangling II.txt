Milestone 2 - Data Wrangling Pipeline

Date: 27 Feb 2026
Reproducible Pipeline and Validation

- What decisions did you make when building the wrangling pipeline?
    - This week we focused on making the entire wrangling pipeline reproducible end-to-end. Every step from raw CSV loading to the final
    analysis_ready.csv can be rerun from scratch by executing the notebook top to bottom with no manual intervention. We achieved this by
    saving intermediate processed CSVs for each indicator (electricity_access_long.csv, gdp_long.csv, etc.) so that downstream notebooks
    do not need to repeat the reshaping steps.
    - We added explicit data quality checks at the end of the pipeline including impossible value detection (percentages outside 0-100),
    urban + rural sum validation, risk_category consistency checks, and a data leakage check. These checks act as assertions — 
    if anything is wrong the output will show it before it reaches modeling.

- What validation steps did you add and why?
    - I added a schema validation table that prints every column name, dtype, null count, and a sample value after saving analysis_ready.csv.
    This gives any reader of the notebook immediate confirmation that the output is clean without having to inspect the CSV manually.
    - I also added a final data quality report printing total rows, columns, countries, year range, and zero-null confirmation.

- What challenges did you face?
    - The most difficult challenge was handling the remaining NaNs after interpolation. A small number of country-year combinations had
    NaNs in engineered features (elec_access_change, gdp_growth, urban_change) because they are the first year for a country and
    differencing produces NaN by definition. We handled these with a second-pass interpolation followed by median fill as a fallback.
    - We also discovered that 2024 rows were appearing in the dataset after interpolation even though I filtered to 1990+ earlier. The
    World Bank CSVs include a 2024 column with preliminary estimates which survived the melt step. I added an explicit year <= 2023
    filter after the quality check to remove these.
